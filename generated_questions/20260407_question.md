## Statistics Question (20260407)

---

#### Problem

A botanist is testing the effectiveness of three different organic fertilizers on the growth of tomato plants. She selects 15 identical tomato seedlings and randomly assigns 5 plants to each of the three fertilizer treatments (Fertilizer X, Fertilizer Y, and Fertilizer Z). After 30 days, she measures the height increase (in centimeters) for each plant. The results are shown below:

| Fertilizer X | Fertilizer Y | Fertilizer Z |
|:------------:|:------------:|:------------:|
| 12.5 | 14.2 | 18.3 |
| 11.8 | 15.1 | 19.2 |
| 13.2 | 13.8 | 17.5 |
| 12.1 | 16.0 | 20.1 |
| 13.4 | 14.5 | 18.8 |

**Questions** (Total: 20 points)

1. **(4 points)** Calculate the mean height increase for each fertilizer group.

2. **(6 points)** Complete the following analysis table by filling in all missing values:

| Source | SS | df | MS | F |
|:-------|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Between Groups | ? | ? | ? | ? |
| Within Groups | ? | ? | ? | |
| Total | ? | ? | | |

3. **(5 points)** Using the F-distribution table below, determine the approximate p-value range for this test.

| df₁ (numerator) | df₂ (denominator) | F₀.₁₀ | F₀.₀₅ | F₀.₀₁ |
|:---------------:|:-----------------:|:------|:------|:------|
| 2 | 12 | 2.81 | 3.89 | 6.93 |

4. **(5 points)** At the 5% significance level, can you conclude that at least one fertilizer produces a different mean growth? Explain your reasoning.

---

#### Solution

**1. Group means**

$$
\bar{x}_X = \frac{12.5 + 11.8 + 13.2 + 12.1 + 13.4}{5} = \frac{63.0}{5} = 12.6
$$

$$
\bar{x}_Y = \frac{14.2 + 15.1 + 13.8 + 16.0 + 14.5}{5} = \frac{73.6}{5} = 14.72
$$

$$
\bar{x}_Z = \frac{18.3 + 19.2 + 17.5 + 20.1 + 18.8}{5} = \frac{93.9}{5} = 18.78
$$

**Group means:** Fertilizer X = **12.6 cm**, Fertilizer Y = **14.72 cm**, Fertilizer Z = **18.78 cm**

---

**2. ANOVA table calculations**

First, compute the grand mean:

$$
\bar{x}_{\text{grand}} = \frac{63.0 + 73.6 + 93.9}{15} = \frac{230.5}{15} = 15.367
$$

**Between-group sum of squares (SS₍BG₎):**

$$
SS_{BG} = \sum_{j=1}^k n_j(\bar{x}_j - \bar{x}_{\text{grand}})^2
$$

$$
SS_{BG} = 5(12.6 - 15.367)^2 + 5(14.72 - 15.367)^2 + 5(18.78 - 15.367)^2
$$

$$
SS_{BG} = 5(-2.767)^2 + 5(-0.647)^2 + 5(3.413)^2
$$

$$
SS_{BG} = 5(7.656) + 5(0.419) + 5(11.649)
$$

$$
SS_{BG} = 38.28 + 2.095 + 58.245 = 98.62
$$

**Within-group sum of squares (SS₍WG₎):**

For Group X:
$$
SS_X = \sum(x_i - 12.6)^2 = (12.5-12.6)^2 + (11.8-12.6)^2 + (13.2-12.6)^2 + (12.1-12.6)^2 + (13.4-12.6)^2
$$
$$
= 0.01 + 0.64 + 0.36 + 0.25 + 0.64 = 1.90
$$

For Group Y:
$$
SS_Y = \sum(x_i - 14.72)^2 = (14.2-14.72)^2 + (15.1-14.72)^2 + (13.8-14.72)^2 + (16.0-14.72)^2 + (14.5-14.72)^2
$$
$$
= 0.2704 + 0.1444 + 0.8464 + 1.6384 + 0.0484 = 2.948
$$

For Group Z:
$$
SS_Z = \sum(x_i - 18.78)^2 = (18.3-18.78)^2 + (19.2-18.78)^2 + (17.5-18.78)^2 + (20.1-18.78)^2 + (18.8-18.78)^2
$$
$$
= 0.2304 + 0.1764 + 1.6384 + 1.7424 + 0.0004 = 3.788
$$

$$
SS_{WG} = SS_X + SS_Y + SS_Z = 1.90 + 2.948 + 3.788 = 8.636
$$

**Total sum of squares:**
$$
SS_T = SS_{BG} + SS_{WG} = 98.62 + 8.636 = 107.256
$$

**Degrees of freedom:**
$$
df_{BG} = k - 1 = 3 - 1 = 2
$$
$$
df_{WG} = N - k = 15 - 3 = 12
$$
$$
df_T = N - 1 = 15 - 1 = 14
$$

**Mean squares:**
$$
MS_{BG} = \frac{SS_{BG}}{df_{BG}} = \frac{98.62}{2} = 49.31
$$
$$
MS_{WG} = \frac{SS_{WG}}{df_{WG}} = \frac{8.636}{12} = 0.7197
$$

**F-statistic:**
$$
F = \frac{MS_{BG}}{MS_{WG}} = \frac{49.31}{0.7197} = 68.52
$$

**Completed ANOVA table:**

| Source | SS | df | MS | F |
|:-------|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Between Groups | **98.62** | **2** | **49.31** | **68.52** |
| Within Groups | **8.636** | **12** | **0.7197** | |
| Total | **107.26** | **14** | | |

---

**3. p-value determination**

From the F-distribution table with df₁ = 2 and df₂ = 12:
- F₀.₁₀ = 2.81
- F₀.₀₅ = 3.89
- F₀.₀₁ = 6.93

Our calculated F = **68.52**, which is much larger than F₀.₀₁ = 6.93.

Therefore, the **p-value < 0.01**.

---

**4. Conclusion**

At the 5% significance level, we **reject the null hypothesis** that all three fertilizers produce the same mean plant growth. 

**Reasoning:** The p-value is less than 0.01, which is much smaller than our significance level of 0.05. The very large F-statistic (68.52) indicates that the variation between the fertilizer group means is substantially larger than what we would expect by random chance alone. This provides strong evidence that at least one fertilizer treatment results in a different mean growth rate compared to the others.

(Note: This test does not tell us *which* specific fertilizers differ from each other—that would require post-hoc pairwise comparisons.)
